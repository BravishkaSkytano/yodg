> [!summary]
> The **upper part of a church’s walls** that contains a row of **windows above eye level**, letting **natural light** into the central space (usually the [[nave]]). Clerestories are common in **[[basilica|Basilica]]s and [[20251012184431|Gothic]] cathedrals**, creating a bright, uplifting atmosphere.

**Example:** The **clerestory windows** of **Chartres Cathedral** in **France**. See [[🪴 The Rose Window of Chartres Cathedral]]
