> [!summary]
> A large, rectangular **building style** originally used in **ancient Rome** for public meetings and later adapted by Christians for churches. It usually has a **long central hall (nave)** with **side aisles**, an [[apse|Apse]] (curved area) at one end, and sometimes a [[clerestory|Clerestory]] with windows high on the walls to let in light.

**Example:** The **Old St. Peter’s Basilica** in **Rome**, built in the 4th century CE.
