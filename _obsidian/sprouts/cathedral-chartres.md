A famous [[20251012184431|Gothic]] cathedral in Chartres, France, built mainly between 1194 and 1250. It is celebrated for its soaring pointed arches, [[Flying Buttress|flying buttress]]es, stained glass windows that tell biblical stories, and sculpted portals with detailed figures of saints and kings. The cathedral exemplifies [[20251012184431|Gothic]] emphasis on height, light, and spiritual awe.

**Example artwork/feature:** The Blue Virgin stained glass window (Notre Dame de la Belle Verrière) is a famous masterpiece of [[20251012184431|Gothic]] art at Chartres.
