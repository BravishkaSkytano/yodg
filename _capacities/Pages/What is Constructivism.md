# What is Constructivism?

When I first read about constructivism, I immediately thought of the Matrix and laughed. Who would really believe that the world that we live in was something like the Matrix? That none of this is real? It sounds crazy to me, what's even the point of believing that? But then, when I was faced with the question "what is reality?" I started to ponder more on constructivism and why someone would adhere to this particular philosophy.

In my own life, I have spent years daydreaming about being in a different world as a different person. I daydreamed to escape. My world was damaged, broken, full of pain, and daydreaming was just a way to get away from it all and find some kind of peace. It's a silly thing we've done as children, but I wasn't a child anymore.

As an adult, I still daydream, and I still have imaginary friends. Why? Because my world is still damaged, broken, and full of pain. Thinking back on how I've felt over the years, I can see why someone would want to believe that none of this is real. Life hurts, and we all make mistakes. Some of us have experienced so much pain and made so many mistakes, that it can feel overwhelming and hopeless. Constructivism whispers that all of this is, somehow, escapable, that none of it is real or matters. With that insight, I now see how constructivism can be so appealing. However, believing a lie only offers temporary comfort and causes more harm than good. So, is reality truly a construct of our minds?
