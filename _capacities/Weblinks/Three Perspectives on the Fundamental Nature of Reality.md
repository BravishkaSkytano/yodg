# Three Perspectives on the Fundamental Nature of Reality

> [[Physicists](../Definitions/Physicism.md) say] that the physical or material reality is ultimately what is real.

> [Idealists](../Definitions/Idealism.md) believe that everything is mental and that the fundamental reality is consciousness.

> [[Social constructionists](../Definitions/Social%20Constructivism.md)] argue that what people claim to be real is found in and framed by how they talk about reality and justify what is real in social contexts, which frames the dialogue in inescapable ways.

> We can think about these three positions as the (1) [objectivists](../Definitions/Objectivism.md) who rely on natural science to determine what is real; (2) [subjectivists](../Definitions/Subjectivism.md) who rely on perceptual experience; and (3) [intersubjectivists](../Definitions/Intersubjectivity.md) who point to the process of social construction and justification to frame reality.

---

To summarize, in order for these three ideologies to be weaved together into one, we would have to acknowledge and accept the following facts:

1. Science has show us the universe has evolved from energy, particles, and atoms, into cells, animals, and people. This points to [Physicism](../Definitions/Physicism.md).

2. We can't directly access the world, we can only use our epistemological worldviews and perceptions to experience it. This points to [Idealism](../Definitions/Idealism.md).

3. All generalizable knowledge must be reasonable and justified facts. Agreeing with either point one or two would be an intersubjective agreement, thus pointing to [Social Constructivism](../Definitions/Social%20Constructivism.md).
