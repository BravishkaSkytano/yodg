# David Barton takes you through the history of Christopher Columbus. #Faith #WallBuilders #History

In this video, Dave Barton from WallBuilders emphasizes the current cultural crisis surrounding the understanding of truth in America, highlighting the alarming trend where many people, including Christians, believe there is no absolute moral truth. He reviews historical figures like [Christopher Columbus](../People/Christopher%20Columbus.md) within the context of recent movements to tear down statues representing controversial figures, advocating for a return to objective truth and the importance of understanding history beyond popular narratives.

1. Introduction to WallBuilders and Historical Context

    1. Dave Barton introduces WallBuilders, explaining its mission to rebuild America's foundations by referencing the biblical story of Nehemiah and stressing the role of grassroots efforts in effecting change.

    1. Truth is on life support. Polling data shows that three-in-five Americans, four-in-five Millennials, and one-in-two Christians believe there is no absolute moral truth. This poses a significant risk to the nation's cohesion.

    3. Truth and fact < personal opinion.

    4. #2Thessalonians 2:10-12 - when we don't love, or reject truth, delusion enters and we will start to believe a lie. Once that happens, we will act on the lie and it will come at a high cost with big consequences.

    5. The ultimate source of truth is the Bible, therefore, we need to read it daily. However, only 9% of Christians do that, even though 65% of Americans claim to be Christians; that means only 6% of Americans read the Ultimate Source of Truth daily.

    6. #John 17:17; 14:6; 8:31-32

3. Impact on Education and Social Norms

    1. Education is examined in light of declining standards, with states creating their own benchmarks leading to a situation where mediocrity is accepted as excellence.

    2. A discussion is held on how truth is often dismissed in favor of personal opinion across topics such as science, history, and economics, exemplifying the degradation of societal norms.

4. The Importance of Loving Truth

    1. Barton quotes scriptures emphasizing the need for Christians to return to a love of truth, noting a significant drop in daily Bible reading among Christians, which contributes to a lack of a biblical worldview.

    2. The consequences of neglecting truth are explored, suggesting that this leads to accepting delusions and lies that have real-world ramifications.

5. Revisiting the History of Columbus

    1. The narrator addresses the removal of Columbus statues, debating the morality of Columbus's actions based on historical accounts that highlight both his contributions and controversies.

    2. The relationship between Columbus and the Taino people is analyzed, emphasizing his positive interactions and the threats posed by other tribes, reframing the narrative around his voyages.

6. The Larger Discourse on Racism and Statues

    1. Barton critiques the recent movement to dismantle statues of figures like Abraham Lincoln or Frederick Douglass, arguing that this goes beyond a simple critique of racism to a broader anti-American sentiment.

    2. He expresses concern over attacks on a variety of historical figures, suggesting that these actions reflect a growing disdain for America’s foundational principles rather than a genuine desire to confront racism.

7. Conclusion and Call to Action

    1. The necessity of defending truth is reiterated, urging individuals to seek out and advocate for historical accuracy and integrity in public discourse.

    2. Barton concludes by encouraging viewers to engage with their history diligently, promoting resources available through WallBuilders to help educate and illuminate the truth for others.
