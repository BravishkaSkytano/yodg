# What is reality? | Christian Apologetics & Research Ministry

> ...it is the totality of all that exists, whether it is known or not known, and that is not dependent on our beliefs or awareness.

> Biblically speaking, reality is the truth of God’s existence and whatever God has created.

> [Empiricism](../Definitions/Empiricism.md) is the idea that all concepts originate with our own experiences and that what we can know is only justified through our experience.

> [Idealism](../Definitions/Idealism.md) is the philosophical perspective that reality is related to and sometimes indistinguishable from human perception.

> [Nominalism](../Definitions/Nominalism.md) is the teaching that universal ideas like redness, roundness, chairness, duckness, etc., are concepts that have no corresponding actuality.  Therefore properties, numbers, and sets are merely different ways of describing whatever exists. Thus, reality exists, but we impose ideas upon it in categories to describe it and understand it.

> [Objectivism](../Definitions/Objectivism.md) is the position that there is a single reality in which we live, the material world and that it exists independent of our awareness.

> [Platonism](../Definitions/Platonism.md) is the view that there are such things as abstract objects that represent reality....They are presupposed.

> [Realism](../Definitions/Realism.md) is a philosophical position that existence is independent of a person’s perception of it.
