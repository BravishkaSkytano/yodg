# Chinese Mantis (Tenodera sinensis)

**Distinguishing sexes:** Begins at L4. Males have longer antennae and more abdominal segments (8 compared to 6). The female's last abdominal segment is larger. Refer to [Distinguishing between male and female mantids](../Zettels/Distinguishing%20between%20male%20and%20female%20mantids.md) for more.

**Behavior:** docile, quiet, and non-aggressive, but can strike a defensive pose.

**Environmental conditions:** temperatures between 22C and 30C, ideally 25C. Night time temperatures can reach as low as 17C. Humidity is relatively normal.

**Reproduction:** refer to [Breeding Mantids](../AtomicNotes/Breeding%20Mantids.md). The [ootheca](../Definitions/ootheca.md) can have 30-100 eggs, and the female will lay 3-7 sacs during her lifetime. Eggs will hatch in around 6 months, as long as the same conditions as the adults are maintained.
