        - #read Documents and Debates in American History and Government: Volume 1, 1493 - 1865

            - Chapter 2 A. Robert Cushman, The Sin and Danger of Self-Love, 1621

            - Chapter 2 C. John Winthrop, "A Model of Christian Charity", 1630

        - Cushman and Winthrop, for example, offered advice to the colonists about how to best prepare themselves mentally and spiritually for the arduous task of a godly commonwealth. Both men urged their audiences to embrace the Christian ideal of “brotherly affection.” In response to the extraordinary demands of colonization, they urged their listeners to willingly be generous and abjure “self-love.”

        - #write three page (including a proper title page) paper in response to the following questions:

            2. What reasons do they offer for these expectations?

            3. Why did Plymouth’s experiment with communal farming fail – and what was it about farming on private property that made it succeed?
