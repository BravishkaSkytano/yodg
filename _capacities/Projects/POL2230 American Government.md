# POL2230: American Government

# Description

This course will look closely at the US Constitution, the Declaration of Independence, and America's founding principles. A study in the workings of the American form of government. Learning the importance of living as responsible citizens in a free society. Reading great literature from the pens of American authors.

## Learning Objectives

1. Apply Biblical principals to politics and identify historic Christian views on politics.

2. Summarize the principles upon which the United States was founded and differentiate among the principles and powers of the Constitutional system of US government.

3. Evaluate competing ideas of justice, equality, and liberty.

4. Analyze and evaluate the major political ideologies, as well as how they afffect contemporary political debates and issues.

5. Understand the importance of a Biblical view of human nature to the American political experience.

6. Identify their political representatives at the federal, state, and local levels.
