# SOC2210: Servant Leadership

# Description

This course helps students take a closer look at Jesus' approach to leading others, emphasizing Christ-like leadership that is linked to moral character, while overserving and analyzing leadership in a local community organization.

## Learning Objectives

1. Articule and differentiate differing approaches to leadership and management.

2. Engagement with the biblical foundations of servant leadership and a type of leadership linked to moral character.

3. Engage in the integration of leaderhsip concepts in one's faith and work.

4. Engage the learner in an intentional plan to develop and grow in leadership confidence and abilities.

    - [ ] A3 Personal Leadership Development Plan April 7, 2025 - May 14, 2025May 9, 2025 - May 21, 2025

        In light of the class readings, lectures and discussions, design a plan for your lifelong development as a Christ-centered, Spirit-filled, biblically informed servant leader. It is critical that you make this plan as comprehensive and clear as you can.

        The idea is to look at yourself and determine what leadership skills you feel you have and why. Then look at where you want to be in life and what leadership skills you will need to have. Then look at what you will need to do to get to the future goal.

        Your plan will include:

        1. Self-Assessment. An assessment of your present strengths and development areas as a leader using the 4R Model, other course materials, and your assessments (StrengthsFinder, Career Codes, Career Direct).

        List the strongest leader skills you see in yourself and cite the specific assessment to support your conclusions – minimum of five.

        2. Top Five Questions. A list of five questions related to your leadership now and in the future that you hope to research more by talking to people and reading, etc. These may arise out of the strengths and development areas you identified in your self-assessment. Include explicit connections to your StrengthsFinder, Career Codes, and Career Direct as appropriate.

        Each question should provide a brief rationale as to what knowledge you want to gain.

        These questions should look at your personal character, present leadership performance, and personal and ministry/professional goals.

        These questions should be specific to you and not general leadership questions.

        3. Three Goals. Based on your work in the above, develop three (3) specific goals that you will endeavor to complete in the next three years. Each goal should be precisely written as a SMART Goal:

        a. Specific

        b. Measurable

        c. Actionable/Attainable

        d. Realistic/achievable

        e. Time-bounded/able to be tracked and monitored

        Break down the goal into specific action steps about how you will make progress toward the end goal.

        Include action steps to achieve each goal with timeline increments of six (6) months, one year, and three years.

        Your plan should be very practical and action oriented. In other words, what am I going to actually do to begin realizing these goals?

        o Sample goal: By June 30 I will identify six possible ministry coaches and interview each. By July 31, I will have researched ten books in my specialized interest and will have chosen two to read. By December 31, I will have read one book.

        o Sample goal: By June 30, I will find a ministry coach to help me learn how to improve as a leader. By December 31, I will identify two emerging leaders to mentor. By the following June 1, will have read two books on my area of specialized interest.

        Based on your questions, develop a list of resources you plan to utilize in the action goals to foster and accelerate your development as a leader. Be thoughtful and creative in selecting diverse (e.g., mentors, books, ministry experiences, retreats, psychometric tools, websites, articles, etc, not just books or websites, and distinct resources (e.g., a particular author is one resource, so please don’t list four of his or her books as separate resources.) Each resource should reflect and have a tight connection to your insights and observations from your self-assessment, goals and questions.

        Format: Three sections, total 500 – 700 words min, Times New Roman, 12-point font, double-spacing, and one-inch margins on all sides. Submit in pdf format to LMS.

        Separate the sections with a header line which includes the name of the section.

        You have 6 weeks to complete. Assigned in L22 and due Wed L30.
