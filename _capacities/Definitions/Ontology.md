# Ontology

A branch of metaphysics that studies beings and their existence.

Comes from the Greek words *onto* and *logos,* meaning the study, or knowledge, of beings, or that which is. It is through this sub-branch of [Metaphysics](./Metaphysics.md) that we find questions such as what is reality? are we living in an illusion? and, how do we know what is real?

---

**Ontology** is a broad and complex field, and there are many different views and theories within it. Here are some of the main views in ontology:

1. **Monism**:

    - **Monistic ontology**: The world can be understood as a single, unified entity or substance.

        - Example: **Spinoza's substance monism**, which posits that God or Nature is the only substance.

    - **Non-monistic ontology**: The world consists of multiple, distinct entities or substances.

        - Example: **Aristotle's hylomorphism**, which posits that reality consists of matter and form.

2. **Dualism**:

    - **Substance dualism**: The world consists of two fundamental substances or types of entities, such as mind and matter.

        - Example: **Descartes' dualism**, which posits that the mind and body are separate substances.

    - **Property dualism**: The world consists of two fundamental types of properties or attributes, such as mental and physical properties.

        - Example: **David Chalmers' property dualism**, which posits that mental properties are fundamental and distinct from physical properties.

3. **Materialism**:

    - **Physicalism**: The world consists only of physical entities and processes.

        - Example: **Marxist materialism**, which posits that the physical world is the only reality.

    - **Eliminativism**: Mental states and processes are not fundamental entities, but rather can be reduced to physical processes.

        - Example: **Paul Churchland's eliminativism**, which posits that mental states are not real entities.

4. **Idealism**:

    - **Subjective idealism**: The world consists only of mental states and processes.

        - Example: **Berkeley's subjective idealism**, which posits that reality is a collection of mental states.

    - **Objective idealism**: The world consists of mental entities or processes, but these are objective and independent of individual minds.

        - Example: **Hegel's objective idealism**, which posits that reality is a dialectical process of mental entities.

5. **Nominalism**:

    - **Nominalist ontology**: The world consists of individual objects or entities, but these are mere labels or names, without any underlying reality.

        - Example: **William of Ockham's nominalism**, which posits that reality consists of individual objects, but these are mere labels.

6. **Realism**:

    - **Realist ontology**: The world consists of independent, objective entities or processes, which exist regardless of human perception or interpretation.

        - Example: **Platonic realism**, which posits that reality consists of abstract, eternal entities.

These are just a few of the main views in ontology, and there are many variations and combinations of these views within the field.
