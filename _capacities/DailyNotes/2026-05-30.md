- Write a cover letter template #To-do

    - Remember to research the company BEFORE you write the CV

    - **INTRODUCTION - the handshake**

        - **2-3 sentences**

        - For example:

            Dear **Hiring Manager**,

            I am excited to apply for the **Job Title** position at **Company Name**. With my experience in **Related Experience or Skill**, I am confident that I can contribute positively to your team.

        1. **Start with a Hook** 🎣 Begin with a sentence that grabs attention and connects you to the role.

        2. **Mention the Job Title and Where You Found It** 📝 
        ​Be clear about which position you’re applying for and how you learned about it.​​​​​

        3. **Show Genuine Excitement About the Company** 🌟 
        ​Highlight what excites you about the company’s mission, values, or recent accomplishments. This shows you’ve done your research.

        4. **Briefly Preview Your Fit for the Role** 🎯 
        ​Provide a quick hint of why you’re the right candidate by referencing a key skill, quality, or achievement.

        5. **Be Positive and Confident** 💪 
        ​Convey a tone of excitement and readiness to contribute from the start. Avoid being overly formal or generic—keep it professional but engaging

        6. For example:

            "🎣 As a dedicated [role or field], I was thrilled to see the opportunity to join [company name] as your [position title].

            📝 I am writing to apply for the [position title] role at [company name], as advertised on [job board/website].

            🌟 Your commitment to [specific value or goal] resonates with me, and I am eager to contribute to your team.

            🎯 With my background in [relevant skill or field], I am confident I can bring value to your team and help achieve [company goal]."

    - **BODY - the evidence**

        - 2-3 short paragraphs

        - focus on skills and EXP that makes you a good hire, even if the XP is limited

        - explain why you're excited for the opportunity

        - mention how the company's values and goals align with your own

    - **CLOSING - the goodbye**

        1. **Express Confidence and Enthusiasm** 💪
        End your cover letter with a sentence that confidently summarizes why you’re a good fit for the role.

        2. **Include a Call to Action 📞**
        Politely invite the employer to take the next step. This shows initiative and reminds them you’re serious about the role.

        3. **Show Appreciation 🙏**
        Thank the employer for taking the time to review your application. A genuine thank-you leaves a positive impression.

        4. **Keep It Concise and Positive 👍**
        The closing statement should be no more than 2-3 sentences. Be direct, professional, and optimistic about what’s next. Avoid repeating information from the rest of your letter.

        5. **Finish with a Polite Sign-Off ✍️**
        Use a professional yet friendly closing phrase like "Sincerely," "Best regards," or "Thank you," followed by your name. This adds a polished and respectful touch.

        6. Example:

            **💪** I’m excited about the opportunity to bring my [specific skill or quality] to [company name] and contribute to [a goal or value of the company].

            📞 I would love the opportunity to discuss how my skills align with the needs of your team. I am available for an interview at your convenience.

            🙏 Thank you for considering my application.

            👍 I look forward to the possibility of contributing to [company name].”

            ✍️ Sincerely, [your name]
