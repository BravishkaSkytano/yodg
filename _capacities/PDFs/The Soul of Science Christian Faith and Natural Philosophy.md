# Part One: The New History of Science

## Ch 1. An Invented Institution: Christianity and the Scientific Revolution

- [Loren Eiseley](../People/Loren%20Eiseley.md) is a science writer who said that [science](../Definitions/science.md) is not something "natural" to man like inquisitiveness is. Scientific thought really started in Western [Europe](../Places/Europe.md) as a result of the [Christian](../Definitions/Christianity.md) world.

> ... scholars generally divided history into three stages-[the ancient world](../Zettels/the%20ancient%20world.md), regarded as brilliant though limited in its scientific understanding; [the medieval world](../Zettels/the%20Middle%20Ages.md), dismissed as a time of intellectual and cultural desolation (the "dark ages"); and [the modern age](../Zettels/the%20modern%20age.md), heralded as a time when reason and enlightenment arose to dispel the mists of medieval superstition. #history

- French physicist and philosopher [Pierre Duhem](../People/Pierre%20Duhem.md) (1861-1916) was the first to begin changing the opinion of development of [science](../Definitions/science.md) during [the Middle Ages](../Zettels/the%20Middle%20Ages.md) while searching for historical examples of his [philosophy of science](../Definitions/philosophy%20of%20science.md). He assumed that the medieval world had nothing to offer, but he was surprised to come across thirteenth-century scientist [Jordanus de Nemore](../People/Jordanus%20de%20Nemore.md) and his work on the foundations of [statics](../Definitions/statics.md), as well as fourteenth-century scientists [Albert of Saxon](../People/Albert%20of%20Saxon.md), [Jean Buridan](../People/Jean%20Buridan.md), and [Nicole Oresme](../People/Nicole%20Oresme.md). #philosophy #history

- [statics](../Definitions/statics.md) -> a branch of mechanics dealing with masses or forces at rest.

- In the late 19th century, several small groups of scientists and scholars in England, under the leadership of [Thomas H. Huxley](../People/Thomas%20H%20Huxley.md), sought to overthrow the intellectual dominance of the Anglican church and replace it with the "church scientific." It was during this period that literature about the supposed hostility of religion against science throughout history emerged from authors such as [John William Draper](../People/John%20William%20Draper.md) and [Andrew Dickson White](../People/Andrew%20Dickson%20White.md). #history #science

    - Draper's book History of the Conflict Between Religion and Science was extremely [anti-Catholicism](../Definitions/anti-Catholicism.md) and was quickly forgotten.

    - White's book A History of the Warfare of Science with Theology, though even more [anti-Catholicism](../Definitions/anti-Catholicism.md), continues to be praised to this day by historians like [George Sarton](../People/George%20Sarton.md) and [Bruce Mazlish](../People/Bruce%20Mazlish.md).

- Many different cultures had a higher level of learning and technology than medieval [Europe](../Places/Europe.md), yet it was [Christianized](../Definitions/Christianity.md) Europe that gave birth to modern science. Even those who were not Christians subscribed to the biblical worldview.

- [science](../Definitions/science.md) can't reach it's true potential within worldviews that feature [pantheism](../Definitions/pantheism.md) or [Idealism](../Definitions/Idealism.md), because those worldviews teach that nature and matter are unreal or illusions. Therefore, nature and matter are often ignored or avoided by pantheists and idealists.
